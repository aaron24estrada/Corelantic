Issue #10 · D1 Open Sans Apple blue corner-shape: squircle
Corners

The squircle is the signature

corner-shape shipped in Chrome 139 and has no public timeline for Safari or Firefox. The radius is the baseline; the superellipse is a progressive enhancement. No polyfill, no dependency, no bug report from a Safari user.

These five ignore the slider so they stay a fixed reference. They are 120px boxes with a 52px radius — a 43% ratio, where the shape reads clearly. That ratio is the thing nobody mentions: on a 400px card with a 20px radius, a squircle and a circle differ by about a pixel. Corners announce themselves on buttons and tiles, not on panels.

Elevation

Buttons that press

An iOS button is a tight contact shadow, a wider ambient one, and a 1px inset highlight along the top edge. On press it darkens, loses the ambient shadow, and scales to 97%.

The button darkens on hover rather than lightening, which is both what iOS does and the only option available to us: #0077ED, the obvious lighter hover, drops white text to 4.32:1. For the same reason the fill carries no gradient — every stop lighter than #0071E3 fails AA. The lift comes from shadow and the inset highlight instead.

Color

Apple's blue, split in two

Every pair below clears WCAG AA. The fill blue and the text blue are different values because one carries white text and the other sits on a light ground — a single blue cannot pass both. Ratios were computed, not eyeballed.

Rejected, and why
#007AFF — iOS system blue the blue most people mean 4.02:1 white text · fails AA
#0A84FF — as a fill fine as text on dark, not a button 3.65:1 white text · fails AA
#0077ED — as a hover lightening on hover breaks AA 4.32:1 white text · fails AA
#86868B — as body text Apple's secondary gray 3.62:1 on white · fails AA

Apple ships all four. They are setting marketing copy; our users are reading a voucher rate off a tile. #86868B survives as a control border, where the 3:1 non-text floor applies instead.
